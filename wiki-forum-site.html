The Smiths & TV Girl

Quick Facts

Active

Origin

This encyclopedia covers two influential bands: The Smiths, the legendary English rock band, and TV Girl, the contemporary indie pop group known for their nostalgic sound and sampling techniques.

TV Girl

TV Girl is an American indie pop band from San Diego, California, consisting of Brad Petering, Jason Wyman, and Wyatt Harmon. The band is known for incorporating samples from a variety of sources into their music, creating a nostalgic, lo-fi sound.

Discography

The band has released several albums and EPs, with their music often featuring themes of relationships, nostalgia, and urban life.

The Smiths

The Smiths
Formed
1982
Disbanded
1987
Members
Morrissey, Johnny Marr, Andy Rourke, Mike Joyce

The Smiths were an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1982. The group consisted of vocalist Morrissey, guitarist Johnny Marr, bassist Andy Rourke and drummer Mike Joyce. They are regarded as one of the most important acts to emerge from the British independent music scene of the 1980s.

Musical Style

The Smiths' music was characterised by Morrissey's distinctive vocals and lyrics, and Marr's jangly guitar work. Their songs often dealt with themes of alienation, social commentary, and relationships.
